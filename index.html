What is Schedule 1?

Schedule 1 drops you into the shoes of an ambitious drug dealer navigating the criminal underworld of Hyland Point. Starting from street-level deals, you'll build a sophisticated operation complete with production labs, distribution networks, and legitimate business fronts to launder your profits.

What sets this game apart is its perfect balance of depth and humor. Behind the cartoon visuals lies a complex simulation that challenges you to manage resources, avoid law enforcement, and outmaneuver rival operations. Whether playing solo or with friends (2-4 player co-op), there's always another operation to plan, another territory to claim, or another cop to evade.

Story & Lore

From Desert to Kingpin: Your Rise in Hyland Point

Your story begins in the sleepy desert town of Serene Flats, where you've been working under your Uncle Nelson out of an RV, growing and dealing pot to the local addicts. It's a quiet life, maybe too quiet for someone with bigger ambitions. But everything changes one night when a police sting hits Nelson's ranch.

The Night Everything Changed

The sirens pierce through the desert night. Cops are swarming the ranch, and you barely make it to the RV before they spot you. Heart pounding, you slam the vehicle in gear and drive—just drive—until the desert gives way to urban sprawl. Welcome to Hyland Point, they say. The scenic cesspit where dreams go to die, but fortunes are made.

You park the RV and think you've made it. You've escaped. You're safe. Then... BOOM. A massive explosion rocks the night. Your RV—your home, your lab, everything—is suddenly a smoldering crater. Standing in the wreckage, you realize this isn't just bad luck. This is a message from rival cartels: "Stay small, or don't stay at all."

The Voice on the Payphone

Just when you think it's over, a payphone rings. It's Uncle Nelson. Somehow, he's evaded the cops and he's got news that changes everything. This isn't the end—it's your beginning. Nelson's been planning this for years. Hidden across Hyland Point are cash stashes, ready for this exact moment. He's not just your uncle; he's your mentor, your guide to becoming something more than a street dealer.

Pro Tip: The Prologue Stash

Before your RV explodes, you can grab your grow setup! Two LED grow lights and pots are worth keeping—you won't get replacement opportunities until much later. This single action can dramatically speed up your early game progress.

Your Path to Power

Hyland Point isn't just a new city—it's a game board, and you're about to learn the rules. From the moment that payphone rings, Nelson becomes your ghostly guide, accessible only through the city's scattered payphones. Each call brings new contacts, new opportunities, and new dangers.

The Six Kingdoms of Hyland Point

Hyland Point isn't just one city—it's six distinct territories, each with its own character, challenges, and opportunities. Your rise from street dealer to kingpin means conquering them all, one rank at a time.

Northtown

Your starting ground, where the standards are low and the money flows steady. Dan's Hardware, the Motel, and countless shady corners make this the perfect training ground.

Rank: Start  Dealer: Benji Coleman  Customer Standards: Low

Westville

The working-class district with lakeside charm. The boardwalk and construction sites offer perfect dealing spots, while the Bungalow becomes your first major property investment.

Rank: Hoodlum 1  Dealer: Molly Presley  Customer Standards: Low-Medium

Downtown

Money laundering central, where legitimate businesses hide dirty cash. The Casino, Mayor's House, and Ray's Realty define this district where appearance matters as much as product.

Rank: Hustler 1  Dealer: Brad Crosby  Customer Standards: Medium-High

Docks

Industrial wasteland meets smuggling haven. The Docks Warehouse offers massive storage capacity, while the waterfront provides discrete shipping opportunities.

Rank: Enforcer 1  Dealer: Jane Lucero  Customer Standards: Medium-High

Suburbia

Verdant neighborhoods hiding desperate housewives and weekend warriors. The money is excellent, but the clientele demands perfection in both product and discretion.

Rank: Block Boss 1  Dealer: Wei Long  Customer Standards: High

Uptown

The final frontier—nightlife, wealth, and endless opportunity. Club scenes and penthouse parties make this the ultimate test of your criminal enterprise.

Rank: Baron 1  Dealer: Leo Rivers  Customer Standards: Premium

The Geography of Power

Each district unlock represents more than just new customers—it's a promotion in Hyland Point's criminal hierarchy. As you progress from Northtown's skate parks to Uptown's exclusive clubs, the product quality demands increase, but so do the profits. By the time you're dealing in Suburbia and Uptown, you're not just a dealer—you're a tastemaker, an influencer in the underground economy.

District Progression Strategy

Don't rush to new districts! Master each area completely—understand customer preferences, optimize your dealer's efficiency, and stockpile quality product. A strong foundation in lower districts makes higher-tier success inevitable.

Characters & NPCs

Pro Tips
  • Keep high-value customers (Very High/High spenders) for direct sales to maximize XP and profits
  • Assign Low-Medium spenders to your dealers for efficient inventory management
  • Match product quality to district standards - Uptown requires Heavenly quality
  • Use the Contacts app on your phone to see relationship levels and connections
  • Always match customers with nearby dealers as distance matters - long travel times slow everything down
  • Assign customers who don't ask for tons of products to dealers - save high-quantity orders for yourself
  • Build relationships with customers connected to dealers to unlock new dealer contacts
  • Offer free samples to new customers matching their favorite effects for better acceptance rates
  • Use the Products app on your phone to list drugs for sale - customers will message you directly
  • Dealers will text you when they're out of inventory - restock them promptly to maintain sales
  • If dealers get stuck, remove all their inventory and wait for their restock text to reset their AI
  • Each dealer can serve up to 8 customers - choose wisely for maximum efficiency

Drug Production

Cannabis Production

The most accessible production method for beginners. Cannabis offers consistent profits with lower risk compared to other drugs.

Basic Setup Requirements
  • Grow Tent ($150)
  • Soil (Standard $20, Long-Life $40, Extra-Life $60)
  • Seeds (from Albert via dead drops)
  • Watering Can
  • Scissors (for harvesting)
Cannabis Soil Types & Growth Cycles
  • Standard Soil - 1 grow cycle
  • Long-Life Soil - 2 grow cycles
  • Extra-Life Soil - 3 grow cycles

Note: Extra-Life soil is the most economical choice for large-scale operations

Cannabis Quality Enhancement Methods

Optimal Cannabis Setup

Efficient 8-Tent Layout

  1. Place 1 medium storage unit as supply shelf
  2. Arrange 2 rows of 4 grow tents facing each other
  3. Position tents 1 unit to the side and 1 unit in front of shelf
  4. Dedicate all pots to same seed type
  5. Assign botanist to the setup
  6. Set botanist to leave raw product on large shelf

Methamphetamine Production

More complex than cannabis, with higher profit potential but increased risk and resource requirements.

Meth Equipment & Process

Required Equipment

  • Chemistry Station - for mixing base chemicals
  • Lab Oven - for crystallizing liquid meth

Meth Production Ingredients

  • Acid (x1) - Found in warehouse
  • Phosphorus (x1) - Found in warehouse
  • Pseudoephedrine (x1) - From Shirley Watts supplier

Meth Production Steps

  1. Mix base at Chemistry Station: Acid + Phosphorus + Pseudo = Liquid Meth
  2. Process Liquid Meth in Lab Oven to create crystallized product

Note: Meth quality depends entirely on pseudo quality

Optimal Meth Production Setup

Efficient Lab Layout

  1. Place Chemistry Station near supply shelf
  2. Position Lab Oven adjacent to Chemistry Station
  3. Set up medium shelf for liquid meth storage
  4. Assign chemist to lab operations
  5. Stock sufficient supplies of acid and phosphorus
  6. Always keep backup pseudo supply for consistent production

Cocaine Production

High-value product requiring cultivation and processing. Unlocked in the Docks district through Salvador Moreno.

Coca Production Process

Required Equipment

  • Grow Tent - for coca plants
  • Chemistry Station - for processing coca leaves
  • Standard growing supplies (soil, watering can, scissors)

Cocaine Production Steps

  1. Grow coca plants in tents (similar to cannabis)
  2. Harvest coca leaves when plants mature
  3. Process leaves at Chemistry Station to create cocaine

Coca Cultivation Tips

  • Use fertilizer and drying racks like cannabis for quality
  • Coca plants grow similar to weed but process differently
  • Final cocaine quality depends on leaf quality
  • Higher profit margins justify premium equipment investment
Optimal Cocaine Production Setup

Efficient Coca Lab Layout

  1. Set up grow area with premium tents and soil
  2. Position Chemistry Station near grow area
  3. Add drying racks for leaf quality improvement
  4. Assign botanist to coca cultivation
  5. Dedicate chemist to leaf processing
  6. Create secure storage for finished product
Advanced Production Strategies
  • Always keep a supply of pseudo as it's not always available
  • Pseudo quality directly influences final meth quality
  • Use mixing stations for custom drug blends and enhanced effects
  • Learn mixing recipes to create valuable variants
  • Coca requires Salvador Moreno supplier (unlock in Docks)
  • Premium cocaine demands justify higher production costs
  • Balance production between products based on district demand
  • Automate with workers for large-scale operations

Worker Management

Recruiting & Managing Workers

Worker Recruitment

  • Recruit from Warehouse District (the barn) after 6 PM
  • Visit the Warehouse District and look for workers in the large barn building
  • Workers have set daily wages and sign-on bonuses
  • Each worker adds to the cost of future workers (+$100 per previous hire, max +$500)

Economic Systems

Understanding the Dual Currency System

Schedule 1 features a sophisticated economy with two distinct monetary forms, each serving specific purposes in your criminal enterprise.

Cash (Dirty Money)

Physical money earned from drug sales, used for illegal goods

Drug Sales  Seed Purchases  Warehouse Goods

Debit (Clean Money)

Digital money for legitimate purchases and businesses

Properties  Vehicles  Equipment

Cash Sources

  • Drug Sales - Primary income source from customer deals
  • Dealer Commissions - Profits from dealer network (you keep 80%)
  • ATM Robbery - Strike ATMs/Cuke machines with charged baseball bat hits
  • Pickpocketing - High-risk income from NPCs
  • Gambling Winnings - Casino payouts (automatically cleaned money)

The ATM Deposit System

Weekly Limits & Restrictions
  • Maximum Deposit: $10,000 per week
  • Reset Schedule: Every Monday
  • Location: Any ATM across Hyland Point
  • Function: Converts cash to debit for legitimate purchases

Money Laundering Businesses

To bypass the $10,000 weekly ATM limit, you need to purchase legitimate businesses for money laundering operations.

Laundering Process

  • Visit the back room of your business
  • Use the PC to select laundering amount
